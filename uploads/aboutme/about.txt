Hi, I'm Pranika.
I grew up in Delhi sketching whatever I was feeling, a habit that quietly turned my notebooks into a decade-long visual diary, where each page is still vivid in my memory. I studied computer science, then spent ten years as a software engineer. I'm now in the open space between that and whatever comes next.

When I was a kid, every adult who met me and my brother delivered the same verdict: “Pranav, so innocent. Pranika, so naughty.” I had usually done nothing. I never quite figured out how they were so sure. Then my niece arrived, with those same eyes, and got accused of mischief for the crime of sitting on a couch. I watched it happen and felt something close to delight. I wouldn't trade it now.

I notice people inside their small joys. A kid losing it at a candy store. A mother dancing with her baby in the corner of a wedding. Two people meeting at airport arrivals after years apart, and the half-second before they reach each other. Most of my favorite moments belong to other people; I'm just lucky enough to be in the room. Lately I've started carrying a camera too. 

For years, I've collected magents from every place I've been (only the strange three-dimensional ones; flat ovals get nothing from me). I enjoy cooking something I've never made before, occasionally, ambitiously, and being genuinely surprised when it works on the first try.I once jumped out of a plane over Key West and couldn't stop thinking about it for months.
